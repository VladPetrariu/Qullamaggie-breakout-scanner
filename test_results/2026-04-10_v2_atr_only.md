## Why it failed

ATR compression as the SOLE primary key selects "dead stocks" not "coiled stocks":

| Problem | % of picks |
|---------|------------|
| EMA stack = "none" (no uptrend) | 59.4% |
| RS percentile < 30 (weak stock) | 46.5% |
| Volume ratio < 0.1 (no trading) | 16.9% |
| ATR < 0.2 (likely dead) | 15.5% |

ATR compression can't distinguish "tight consolidation after a strong move" from "dead stock with no activity." The original quintile analysis showed ATR compression working because it was measured within a pool of stocks that already had some quality. As the sole ranking key, it grabs the quietest stocks in the universe — mostly weak, trendless names.

## Lesson learned

ATR compression is a necessary but not sufficient condition. A tight base needs an uptrend context (EMA stack, RS) to be meaningful. Without quality gates, the tightest stocks are dead stocks.
