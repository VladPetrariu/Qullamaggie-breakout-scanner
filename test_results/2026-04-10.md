## Key Takeaways

1. **ATR compression is the scanner's best signal.** Tight bases predict positive forward returns with a 3%+ spread. This aligns with Qullamaggie's emphasis on tight consolidation patterns.

2. **Catalyst freshness and volume ratio are anti-predictive.** The scanner catches stocks AFTER the catalyst move, and freshness being the primary ranking key means the most extended stocks rank highest. This is the single biggest drag on performance.

3. **The scanner works as a filter but not as a ranker.** It successfully identifies stocks in consolidation patterns near breakout levels (the universe filtering works). But the 3-tier ranking sort promotes the wrong stocks.

4. **Extreme outliers are present.** Returns of +1,124% and -94% suggest some picks experienced corporate events (mergers, delistings, etc.) that skew the averages. A winsorized or trimmed analysis would give a cleaner picture.

## Recommended Changes

1. **Re-weight ranking:** Replace freshness as primary sort key with ATR compression. Use factor evidence to weight the ranking.
2. **Add extension filter:** Penalize stocks that are too many ABRs above their consolidation base. The current system rewards proximity to breakout level but doesn't penalize already-extended stocks.
3. **Post-catalyst cooldown:** Instead of ranking fresh catalysts highest, require stocks to reconsolidate after a volume spike before promoting them.
4. **Winsorize returns in analysis:** Cap extreme outliers (e.g., at +/- 30%) to prevent corporate events from distorting statistics.
