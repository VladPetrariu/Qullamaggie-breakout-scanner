How This Scanner Works

This scanner finds momentum breakout setups across ~2,300 NYSE/NASDAQ stocks using Kristjan Kullamägi's (Qullamaggie) methodology. It does not give buy/sell signals — it organizes raw data so you can make your own decisions.

Market Regime (top banner)

The regime is a broad market health check based on 5 breadth indicators. It determines how aggressively you should trade:

  • Favorable — Most stocks are in uptrends, VIX is low, breakouts are following through. Full confidence to trade setups.
  • Mixed — Market is split. Some sectors strong, others weak. Be selective, focus on leading sectors.
  • Caution — Breadth is deteriorating. Only trade the highest-conviction setups with the best factors.
  • Risk Off — Market is in crisis. No setups are shown. Cash is the position.

The 5 indicators: % of stocks above 50-day MA, % above 20-day MA, new 52-week highs vs lows ratio, VIX level and direction, and recent breakout follow-through rate.

Sector Rotation

Shows which sectors have the most stocks near all-time highs and catalyst activity. Based on the top 50 ranked stocks that have profile data. Brighter green = more breakout activity in that sector. Focus your attention on leading sectors.

Chart (on each card)

60-day candlestick chart with 3 EMA lines:

  • Blue — 10-day EMA (fast)
  • Yellow — 20-day EMA (medium)
  • Purple — 50-day EMA (slow)
  • Dashed line — breakout level (resistance)

Green candles = up day, red = down day. Volume bars along the bottom.

Catalyst

  • Freshness score (0-1) — How fresh the catalyst is. Combines age decay (how many days ago) with extension (how far price has already moved). Higher = fresher catalyst with more room to run.
  • Early (0.75-1.0) — Fresh catalyst, minimal extension
  • Active (0.5-0.75) — Still developing
  • Late (0.25-0.5) — Most of the move likely done
  • Dead (<0.25) — Catalyst exhausted
  • Vol spike — How many times above the 20-day average volume the catalyst day was. Higher = more significant event. "(earnings)" label means the spike coincided with an earnings report.

Volume

  • Volume pace — Today's volume vs 50-day average. >3x = strong, >5x = extreme.
  • Flag volume — Volume during the last 5 days vs the 5 days before. <0.5x (dry up) is ideal — means volume is contracting during consolidation, building energy for the breakout.

Relative Strength (RS)

  • RS percentile — How this stock's 20-day ATR-normalized return ranks vs the full ~2,300 stock universe. 90th+ percentile = top 10% strongest stocks.
  • RS direction — Change in RS percentile over the last 5 days. Rising RS (green arrow) is the most actionable signal.
  • vs SPY — Stock's 20-day return minus SPY's 20-day return. Positive = outperforming the market.
  • vs Sector — Same comparison but against the stock's sector ETF. Tells you if it's leading within its sector, not just riding a sector wave.

Consolidation

  • EMA stack — Whether price is above the 10, 20, and 50 EMAs in order. Full (price > 10 > 20 > 50) = strongest uptrend. Partial/weak = less alignment.
  • ATR compression — Short-term volatility / long-term volatility. Values below 0.7 mean the stock is coiling tightly — potential energy building for a move. Lower = tighter = better.
  • Consolidation days — How long the stock has been consolidating. 1-2 weeks = strong, 3-4 weeks = very strong, 1-3 months = powerful base.
  • HH/HL % — Percentage of consolidation days with higher-highs AND higher-lows. 70%+ = strong upward drift during consolidation.
  • Candle quality — Average body-to-range ratio during consolidation. "Ideal" = clean bodies, "barcode" = all wicks (wide stops, harder to trade).
  • Pole magnitude — If the stock had a sharp up-move (>20%) before consolidating. This is the classic pole+flag pattern.

Breakout Level

  • ATH — All-time high. No overhead supply, maximum conviction.
  • Multi-Year — 2-5 year high. Minimal supply above.
  • 52wk High — 52-week high. Solid but some overhead.
  • Prior Resist. — Previous resistance level. Weakest — needs strong factors to compensate.
  • ATR to level — How far the current price is from the breakout level, measured in ATR units. Closer to 0 = near the level. Negative = already above it.

Weekly Confluence

Whether the daily and weekly charts are confirming each other:

  • Max Confluence — Both daily and weekly charts are coiling simultaneously
  • Max Confirmation — Both daily and weekly are breaking out simultaneously
  • Strong — Weekly coiling while daily is breaking out
  • Context — Weekly uptrend supports the daily setup but isn't confirming

Profile

  • ABR — 21-day Average Body Range. The average |Close - Open| in dollars. Used to normalize distances and estimate risk.
  • Float — Shares available for trading. Micro (<10M) = can move explosively. Large (>100M) = needs institutional buying to move.
  • Short interest — % of float sold short. High SI (>25%) with a strong setup = potential squeeze amplifier.

Ranking

Stocks are sorted by a 3-tier system:

  • Primary — Catalyst freshness score (freshest catalysts first)
  • Secondary — Factor quality count (how many factors are in their "strong" range)
  • Tertiary — ABR distance to breakout level (closer to level = higher)

Filters & Controls

  • EMA stack filter — "Full stack only" is the default, showing only stocks in the strongest uptrend configuration
  • Catalyst filter — "With catalyst" shows only stocks with a recent volume spike. "Fresh catalyst" limits to early/active freshness.
  • Level filter — Filter to ATH-only, or include multi-year and 52-week highs
  • Sort — "Rank" uses the 3-tier sort. Other options sort by a single factor.
  • Keyboard — Press / to focus the search bar

Historical Badges

  • NEW — Stock was not on yesterday's watchlist
  • ▲N / ▼N — Stock moved up/down N positions in rank vs yesterday

Track Record

When you have scans from multiple days, a "Track Record" banner appears showing win rate and average return at 1, 3, 5, and 10-day horizons for the top 20 ranked stocks from each prior scan. This tells you how well the scanner's picks have historically performed.

Watch Mode

Run python -m scanner --watch to monitor your watchlist during market hours. It polls prices every 2 minutes and sends a notification when a stock approaches or breaks its breakout level.
